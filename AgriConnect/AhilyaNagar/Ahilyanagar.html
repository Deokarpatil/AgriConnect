Agriculture in Ahilyanagar

Ahilyanagar is a very large district in the center of Maharashtra. It has many villages and a large number of farmers. Agriculture (शेती) is the main occupation of the people here. Because the climate is hot and dry, and rainfall is low and irregular, farming here is very challenging. Still, the farmers of Ahilyanagar work very hard and grow many types of crops with great care and planning.

In earlier days, only traditional crops like jowar (ज्वारी), bajra (बाजरी), and pulses like tur (तूर) and moong (मुग) were grown. These crops do not need a lot of water and can survive in dry weather. But in the last few decades, with the help of irrigation projects (सिंचन योजना), modern farming methods, and government schemes, farmers started growing high-value crops like sugarcane (ऊस), onion (कांदा), pomegranate (डाळिंब), grapes (द्राक्ष), and more. Now, Ahilyanagar is becoming a leading agricultural district in Maharashtra.

Major Crops Grown in Ahilyanagar

Ahilyanagar has a very diverse cropping pattern. This means many different types of crops are grown in different seasons.

1. Food Grains and Pulses (धान्य आणि कडधान्य)

Jowar (ज्वारी) and Bajra (बाजरी) are the main grains. They are hardy and grow even if there is less water.

Tur (तूर डाळ) and Moong (मुग डाळ) are important pulses.

These crops are mostly grown during the rainy season and do not need much care.

2. Cash Crops (नगदी पिके)

Cotton (कापूस) is grown widely in medium black soil.

Soybean (सोयाबीन) is also becoming popular among farmers.

These crops give farmers good income when sold in the market.

3. Sugarcane (ऊस)

Sugarcane is one of the most important crops in Ahilyanagar.

It needs a lot of water, so it is grown in places with good irrigation.

The district has many co-operative sugar factories, which buy sugarcane from farmers and help them earn better income.

4. Vegetables (भाजीपाला)

Onion (कांदा) is a major vegetable. Ahilyanagar is one of the top onion-producing areas in Maharashtra.

Other vegetables: Tomato (टोमॅटो), Brinjal (वांगी), Chilli (मिरची), Peas (मटार).

These are grown under irrigation, especially near wells and canals.

5. Fruits (फळपिके)

Important fruits grown here: Pomegranate (डाळिंब), Mango (आंबा), Guava (पेरू), Chikoo (चिकू), Kagzi Lime (कागदी लिंबू), Grapes (द्राक्ष).

These crops give higher profits if taken care of properly.

Many farmers now grow orchards with drip irrigation and proper guidance from KVK and agricultural universities.

Irrigation and Farming Methods

Ahilyanagar gets about 400–600 mm of rain each year, which is not enough for all crops. So, irrigation (सिंचन) is very important.

Only about 26% of the farmland has irrigation.

Most of the irrigation comes from wells and borewells (विहीर, बोअरवेल).

Some areas also use canals (कालवे) from the Pravara, Mula, and Godavari projects.

Farmers use drip systems, sprinklers, and lift irrigation (लिफ्ट सिंचन).

Many still use traditional methods, but modern tools like tractors, threshers, and sprays are now common.

Soil and Climate of Ahilyanagar

Climate (हवामान)

Summers: Very hot (March to May), temperature goes above 40°C.

Monsoon: June to September. Rain is not regular.

Winters: Cool and dry.

Soil Types (मातीचे प्रकार)

Shallow grey sandy soil (हलक्या राखाडी मातीतली माती):

  • Covers the largest area.
  • Not very good at holding water.
  • Good for drought crops like jowar, bajra, pulses.

Medium to deep black cotton soil (मध्यम ते खोल काळी माती):

  • Holds moisture well.
  • Found mostly in irrigated areas.
  • Best for sugarcane, cotton, pomegranate.

Historical Growth and Achievements

Traditional Farming

In olden days, farmers mainly grew crops for their family.

Very little use of machines or fertilizers.

Green Revolution and Irrigation Expansion

After the 1960s, farmers got better seeds and fertilizers.

Mula Dam, Pravara Canal and other projects brought water to many villages.

This helped farmers grow wheat, sugarcane, and cotton.

Co-operative Movement

Many co-operative sugar factories started in this region.

Farmers became members and got support for seeds, fertilizers, and money.

Dairy co-operatives (दुग्ध सहकारी संस्था) also helped improve income.

Education and Research

Mahatma Phule Krishi Vidyapeeth (MPKV), Rahuri is a major university in Ahilyanagar.

It helps farmers with:

  • New seeds
  • Fruit and vegetable research
  • Organic farming and drip irrigation

Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK), Babhaleshwar gives farmer training, demo farms, and on-field guidance.
